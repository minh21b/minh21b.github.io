Introduction

The diamond industry is one of the most lucrative markets globally, yet pricing remains a complex aspect for consumers and industry professionals. This project aims to uncover the factors influencing diamond pricing using the diamonds dataset from the ggplot2 package in R. Despite being widely recognized as a valuable commodity, the pricing of diamonds often seems arbitrary and subjective. Consumers face challenges in understanding the factors driving diamond prices, while industry professionals grapple with pricing strategies amidst varying market dynamics. This project seeks to create interactive visualizations to help consumers understand the effects of certain diamondsâ€™ characteristics on their pricing easier. The primary objective of this project is to analyze the diamonds dataset and create visualizations to uncover the relationships between various attributes of diamonds (such as carat, cut, color, clarity, etc.) and their corresponding prices. By doing so, we aim to provide insights into the factors driving diamond pricing, thus enabling both consumers and industry professionals to make more informed decisions.
